# Chatting in a Space

Once you've [joined a space](/articles/joining-a-space), most of your time there happens in its rooms — the channels described in [Spaces and Rooms Explained](/articles/spaces-and-rooms-explained). Text rooms work like real-time chat: pick a room from the navigation, type in the composer at the bottom, and send. This article covers the everyday mechanics of posting and interacting with messages once you're in one.

## Replying, quoting, and mentioning

Conversations in a busy room move fast, so Flotilla gives you a few ways to keep things clear:

- **Reply** to a specific message to attach your response to it, so anyone reading later can follow the thread instead of guessing what you're responding to.
- **Quote** a message to reference it — handy when you want to bring something up again later, or point to it from a different context.
- **Mention** another member by name to call their attention to a message directly; mentioning someone can trigger a notification for them, depending on their [notification settings](/articles/notifications-and-muting).
- **Mention a room** to link directly to it from inside a message — room mentions are clickable, so anyone reading can jump straight there instead of hunting through navigation.

## Reactions, edits, and deletes

You can react to any message with an emoji instead of sending a full reply — useful for quick acknowledgment without adding noise to the room.

Messages you've sent are yours to manage: you can edit a message after posting it (to fix a typo or clarify something) or delete it outright if you change your mind. Neither affects the rest of the conversation — replies and quotes that reference the message stay intact.

## Sharing images and media

You don't have to stick to text. Flotilla supports sharing images and other media a few different ways:

- **Drag and drop** a file straight into the composer.
- **Paste** an image from your clipboard — including native clipboard paste on mobile.
- **Upload** a file directly using the composer's upload option.

Images are compressed and uploaded to a media server before they're attached to your message, so large images won't necessarily post at full size.

## How rich content renders

Rooms don't just render plain text. As you scroll a room, you'll see:

- **Link previews** for URLs shared in a message.
- **Inline images and video** rendered directly in the chat instead of as bare links.
- **Code blocks** for anything wrapped in backticks — useful if your space is technical.
- **Custom emoji**, if your space has any configured.
- **Sensitive-content blur** — media flagged as sensitive is blurred by default until you choose to reveal it.

## Knowing your message was actually delivered

Because a room lives on a relay rather than a company's server, sending a message isn't quite as simple as it looks. Next to each message you send, Flotilla shows a small per-relay publish-status indicator. It tells you whether the message actually reached the relay and was accepted, rather than silently failing somewhere along the way. If you ever see a message that looks like it didn't go through, that indicator is the first place to check — it's the difference between "posted" and "looked like it posted."

# Using Voice and Video Calls

*Part of our guide to [running an online community you actually own](/articles/how-to-run-an-online-community).*

Some Rooms in a Space are voice Rooms instead of text Rooms — live audio, with video and screen sharing available once you're in. They work like any other Room type: you'll find them in the sidebar alongside the text Rooms, marked with a different icon. This article covers joining and using one as a member. If you're setting up voice and video on a relay you run, see [Enabling Voice and Video Calls on Your Relay](/articles/enabling-voice-and-video-calls-on-your-relay) instead; if you just want the bigger picture of how Rooms fit into a Space, see [Spaces and Rooms Explained](/articles/spaces-and-rooms-explained).

## Joining a Voice Room

Click a voice Room and you'll see a "Join voice room?" dialog before you're actually connected. If people are already in the call, you'll see their profile circles at the top, so you know who you're about to join. Below that, pick a microphone from a dropdown (it defaults to your system's default mic), or check "Join without microphone" if you just want to listen — you can unmute later. "Join voice" connects you; "Don't join" backs out without joining.

## Once You're In

After you join, a small call widget appears and stays visible while you browse the rest of the Space, so you don't have to keep a call screen open just to stay connected. It shows whether you're joining, connected, or disconnected, along with the Room and Space name, and gives you a handful of controls:

- **Mute/unmute** your microphone.
- **Turn your camera on or off.**
- **Share your screen** — available in the browser and on desktop, though not in the native mobile apps.
- **Call settings**, a gear icon that opens a dialog for switching your microphone, speaker, or camera mid-call, in case you plugged in headphones or want to swap devices without leaving the call.
- **Leave the room**, which disconnects you.

There's also a chat toggle so you can read and post in the Room's text chat while staying in the call.

## Adding Video

Turning your camera on (or sharing your screen) adds a video tile for you, and you'll see tiles for anyone else doing the same. With just one or two video tiles active, they're shown full-size, stacked. Once there are more than two, Flotilla switches to a grid so everyone fits.

If you want to focus on one person — a screen share, a presenter, whoever's talking — click the pin icon in the corner of their tile to spotlight it. The spotlighted tile takes over the main view, and everyone else's tiles shrink into a strip alongside it. Click the pin again (it now reads "Exit spotlight") to go back to the grid.

## If a Space Doesn't Have Voice Rooms

Voice and video calls only work on a relay that's been set up to support them. If a Space you're in has no voice Rooms at all, that's a choice (or a gap) on the relay's side, not something wrong with your setup — nothing to troubleshoot on your end. Some Spaces run entirely on text Rooms and never add voice.

## Who Turns This On

Enabling calls is the Space's owner's job, not something a member configures. If they're self-hosting their relay, the steps are in [Enabling Voice and Video Calls on Your Relay](/articles/enabling-voice-and-video-calls-on-your-relay). If their Space is on Coracle Hosting, video calling is a plan-gated toggle in the hosting dashboard — see [Managing Your Hosted Relay](/articles/managing-your-hosted-relay) for where that lives. Either way, if you think a Space should have voice but doesn't, that's a conversation to have with whoever runs it.

If you get stuck, [reach out to the community](/articles/community-and-support) for help.
